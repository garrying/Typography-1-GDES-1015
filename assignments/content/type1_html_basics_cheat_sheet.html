HTML Basics Cheat Sheet for Typography 1

Basic HTML Structure

<!doctype html>
<html>
  <head>
    <title>website title</title>
  </head>
  <body>
     . . . content of website . . .
  </body>
</html>

Each HTML document requires a set of tags that set up the basic aspects that will be read by the browser. Most HTML tags will be place between the <body> tags.

Document Structure

<html> . . . </html>

contains all other HTML elements

<head> . . . </head>

contains all the head elements (e.g. title, link, style)

<title> . . . </title>

defines a title in the browser toolbar

<body> . . . </body>

contains all the contents of an HTML document, such as text, links, images, tables, lists, etc.

Document Info

<meta>

typically specifies page description, keywords, author of the document, last modified, and other metadata

<link>

links to external style sheets

<style> . . . </style>

defines style sheet inside the HTML document

<script> . . . </script>

contains scripting statements, or it points to an external script file

Formatting

<h1> . . . </h1>

heading (h1 for largest to h6 for smallest, eg h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, h6)

<b> . . . </b>

bold text

<i> . . . </i>

italic text

<sup> . . . </sup>

superscript - smaller text placed below normal text

<sub> . . . </sub>

subscript - smaller text placed below normal text

<small> . . . </small>

small - fineprint size text

<pre> . . . </pre>

pre-formatted Text

<blockquote> . . . </blockquote>

text block quote

<strong> . . . </strong>

strong - shown as bold in most browsers

<em> ... </em>

emphasis - shown as italics in most browsers

Section Divisions

<div> . . . </div>

division or section of page content

<span> . . . </span>

section of text within other content

<p> . . . </p>

paragraph of text

<br>

line break

<hr>

basic horizontal line

Images

<img src="url" alt="text">

basic image
(e.g. <img src="https://www.ocadu.ca/logo.png" alt="ocadu logo">)

Linking Tags

<a href="url"> . . . </a>

basic link
(e.g. <a href="https://www.ocadu.ca/">Visit our site</a>)

Lists

<ol> . . . </ol>

Ordered List

<ul> . . . </ul>

Un-ordered List

<li> . . . </li>

List Item (within ordered or unordered)

<dl> . . . </dl>

Definition List

<dt> . . . </dt>

Term or phrase being defined

<dd> . . . </dd>

Detailed Definition of term

Semantic Elements

<header> . . . </header>

Specifies the webpage header. Could also be used for objects inside the webpage.

<footer> . . . </footer>

Specifies the webpage footer. Could also be used for objects inside the webpage.

<main> . . . </main>

Marks the main content of the webpage.

<article> . . . </article>

Denotes an article.

<aside> . . . </aside>

Denotes content displayed in a sidebar of the webpage.

<section> . . . </section>

Specifies a particular section in the webpage.

<figure> . . . </figure>

A tag reserved for figures (diagrams, charts) in HTML5.

<figcaption> . . . </figcaption>

A description of the figure is placed inside these.

<mark> . . . </mark>

Used to highlight a particular portion of the text.

<nav> . . . </nav>

Navigation links for the user in a webpage.

<menuitem> . . . </menuitem>

A particular item from a list or a menu.

<time> . . . </time>

Tag for formatting date and time.

<wbr>

A line-break within the content.

Commonly Used Special Characters

&quot;

Inch Marks " . . . ”

&ldquo; &rdquo;

Quote Marks (left and right) “ . . . ”

&lt; &gt;

Less than and greater than sign < >

&nbsp;

Non-breaking space

&copy; &trade; &reg;

Copyright, trademark, registered symbols © ™ ®

&amp;

Ampersand &

&Uuml;

At symbol @

&ouml;

Small bullet •
